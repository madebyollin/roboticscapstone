Blog Post #3 Part 2

Roles & Responsibilities

Per-Person Designations:

We have distributed the roles based on the interests of each member. Most of the roles will be shared by two members or more, where their knowledge can be complementary. In some cases one of the members will be more experienced which will help the second member expand their knowledge.

Role Team Members Responsibilities
ROS Gurus Kennan, Yifan The leading roles for ROS programming, they will understand all the basics of ROS, and they will commit the codes. They need to effectively communicate with the other members, especially the user interface and perception gurus.
Perception Gurus Ollin, Kevin, Kennan Will work on the perception part and the software to process the sensor data.
Hardware Design and Fabrication Guru Ghada The hardware and design and fabrication have been combined in a single role. The role is to design and make all the parts that will potentially be included to the robot.
User Interface Gurus Ghada, Ollin They will work on the visual and vocal interface of the robot. This is particularly important since we are working on an accessibility robot.
Manager (Will switch between people each week) The manager will make sure that the work is progressing well and that the tasks are evenly shared between team members.
Documentation & Note-Taking Ollin, Kennan, Ghada Will take notes and write blog posts.
Presentations (Will switch between people each time) Will present the results of our work.

Strategies For Balancing Learning Goals:

All members of the team are interested in learning ROS, therefore they will all participate in the ROS programming part; the ROS gurus will be there to guide and help them. This way we will ensure that all members learn ROS.

The team roles will still be flexible along the weeks, if one member wants to work more in-depth on another part.
